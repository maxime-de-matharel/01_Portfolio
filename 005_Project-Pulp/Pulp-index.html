PULP (ex Blacksheep)

October-December 2019
6 min read

Blacksheep (initial name of the company) was created in 2018 by 3 HEC students, with the aim to simplify lunch break by creating a digital menu ordering system. Arnaud, Olivier, and Antoine, the 3 founders, wanted to help restaurant stop losing customers due to the waiting time.
Eagerness is now the norm. It has become impossible to take your time. You see it every day: your customers are getting impatient. Some leave your facility because of the queue. Many will never come back.

THE OFFER

To solve this problem, Blacksheep offer restaurant a simple solution that enables their customers to simply order online in the restaurant - directly on their smartphones.

When I arrived at Blacksheep, the product was a personalized web-app website deployed for each restaurant. You can visit Elgi web-app if you liked to see what it's look like. At the time, it was an online platform accessible to the customers by a QR code so that he could order when in the queue. Blacksheep would display print in front of and into the restaurant in order to make it more visible from the customers.

BRAND IDENTITY

I was recruited in the aim to start designing a product according to a design charter that a design agency did for them. Under this, you will see what looks like the charter. The main colors was a Caribbean green #00EEB2 very contrasting with a #000000 black.

GOALS

As my first mission as Product Owner, I had to understand our users needs and define the High Expectation Customer so that we focus on the needs of peoples that really need the product.  As I interviewed around 10 recurrent users, and identified that they wanted to have an app where they could find all the restaurant where they could use the solution, my second mission was to propose a design for a B2C application. Once a design proposed, I was in charge to organized development sprint and, in the same time the acquisition of new users and arrange the beta launch of the new application. My last mission was to set up Blacksheep on the road to find product/market fit and define Key Performance Indicators.

PRODUCT OWNER

My role in the team was to organize and manage the Product Backlog so that the development team have all the elements they need to start develop the app. As Product Owner, my objective is to make concrete the vision of the product inspired by the founders. I am the common link between development teams, users, and business. I define the order in which the features will be developed and take the necessary orientations express in sprint objective.

RESEARCH & RESULTS

First, I ran couples of interviews to understand our user, my main goals during these interviews was to define our High Expectation Customer, customer that are really happy with a product and that would get sad with they could not use the product anymore. With choose to focus on those customers because we believe that to create a good product and reach product/market fit, the product should solve a real problem. Once we define our HXC, we asked them how they would like to interact with the product, what was their biggest frustration using it and what they would like to be able to do with our product.
We also send them a survey in order to get additional insight from those who did not have time to realize an interview with us.
The main issue we understood from those interview and survey is that the user has memorized the url of the web-app, and order their meal while going to the restaurant or when getting out of their office. We also learned that the user wanted to be albe to see all the restaurant where they could use our web-app. The last learning with get from those interview is that the user was not making any differences between Blacksheep (our identity) and the restaurant identity, they though that the web-app was from the restaurant.
Once we defined the pain and frustration of our persona, I defined a Job To Be Done statement so that all the team have a clear idea of what problem we want to solve with the product.

That is when we realize that we should create a B2C application so that our user could order in anyone of our restaurant in the same app, and by the way be more aware of our brand and identity.
As we went for a product B2C, Blacksheep needed to change name for one more catchy for the user. That is when Pulp came up.
Now that I knew what problem's the users are facing, I could start working on the solution. I started by doing couples of low-fidelity wireframe and test it with Olivier, CTO. Once Olivier and agreed on the structure of the new product, I started the mid-fidelity wireframe and then jumped to the high-fidelity using the colors from the new design charter (above).
